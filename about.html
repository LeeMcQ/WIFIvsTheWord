Our Story

It Began With a Sermon — and a Family

Wi-Fi vs The Word was not born in a boardroom. It was born at a pulpit, preached by a family who lives the question it asks.

One Sabbath, a family stood together to preach a sermon titled The Wi-Fi vs The Word. It asked a single, searching question of every home in the room: is the Wi-Fi winning… or is the Word?

The response was not polite applause. It was recognition. Parents saw their own lounges in it — everyone present in body, everyone absent on a screen. The sermon had named something families were feeling but had not found words for. And from that recognition, a ministry was born.

"Choose this day whom you will serve. As for me and my house, we will serve the Lord." — Joshua 24:15
What We Believe

The convictions that shape everything

Technology is a tool

We are not anti-technology. A tool can build an altar or dig a grave — the question is never the device, but who is discipling whom.

The home is the battleground

Character is formed at the family table, in the ordinary evenings. Restore the home, and you restore the future.

Grace, not guilt

Elijah repaired a broken altar before fire fell. We meet families where they are, with hope — never with shame.

Biblically grounded, scientifically informed

Scripture leads. But we also take seriously what neuroscience reveals about attention, dopamine and the developing mind.
