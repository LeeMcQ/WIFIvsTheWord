The Distraction Dilemma

Is the Wi-Fi winning… or the Word?

We are not anti-technology. Technology is a tool. But the same device that connects us to the world can quietly disconnect us from each other — and from Christ.

Technoference

"Absent presence" — we are in the room with our family, but our minds are pulled away by every notification.

The Dopamine Loop

Endless scrolling and the chase for "likes" rewires the brain — feeding anxiety, restlessness and spiritual numbness.

The Algorithm

Feeds are not neutral. They are custom-built around your weaknesses — a mirror of your secret attention.

Family Fragmentation

The family gathered, yet divided — each person on their own device, in their own world.

The AI Companion Trap

Teens forming bonds with bots that never push back — isolating them from real connection and God's guidance.

The Loss of Deep Work

Brains trained for 15-second videos struggle with 15 minutes in the Word. Attention is a discipleship issue.

Rebuilding the Family Altar

Seven stones to rebuild a tech-wise home

Before fire fell on Mount Carmel, Elijah first repaired the broken altar. We don't only remove what is harmful — we replace it with what is holy. Build it stone by stone, evening by evening.

i

A Sacred Table & Phone-Free Zones

Create a "phone basket" for mealtimes and charge devices outside the bedroom. Protect rest, intimacy and conversation.

ii

The Digital Sabbath

From sundown Friday to sundown Saturday, set the devices aside completely. Fast from the digital so you can feast on the Word.

iii

Replace, Don't Just Remove

Starve the digital escape by feeding the relationship. Use your phone as a tool for mission — send a prayer, not a complaint.

iv

The "Flee" Principle

Like Joseph, don't debate temptation — switch off, block, delete. An open-door policy of honesty guards the marriage covenant.

Genesis 39
v

Wait Until 8th — or Later

Delay the smartphone as long as possible. The developing brain isn't ready for the dopamine. A simple phone can still call home.

vi

The Renewing of the Mind

God is the highest novelty — "taste and see that the Lord is good." In Christ we become a new creation; the mind can be reset.

2 Corinthians 5:17
vii

Apply the Ultimate Filter

Run an "algorithm cleanse." If content fails the Philippians 4:8 test — is it true, lovely, holy? — mark it "Not Interested" and scroll past.

Philippians 4:8
