Honest Questions

Questions Families Often Ask

Straight, gracious answers about the ministry, the message, and living it out at home.

Not at all. Technology is a tool, and a good one. We ask only whether it is building the family altar or quietly replacing it. We help families use technology with intention rather than be used by it.

It is setting devices aside completely for one day each week — typically sundown Friday to sundown Saturday — so the family can rest, worship and reconnect. You fast from the digital so you can feast on the Word.

The ministry was born in an Adventist context and draws on that heritage, but the message — guarding the mind, restoring the family, refocusing on Christ — is for every Christian home. Churches of all backgrounds are warmly welcome to use the material.

It is never too late. Elijah repaired a broken altar before the fire fell. Start with one stone — one phone-free meal, one quiet evening — and build from there. Grace, not guilt, is the foundation.

There is no single right age, but the growing "Wait Until 8th" movement encourages delaying smartphones until at least Grade 8, and many families wait longer. The developing brain is not ready for the dopamine pull. A simple call-and-text phone can meet genuine safety needs. Our Smartphone Readiness Check can help you weigh your own situation.

Guarding the avenues of the soul is not strictness — it is love. You would not let a stranger into your lounge to shout at your children. The goal is not a locked-down home but a home so warm and engaging that the screen loses its grip.

Everything is free. The full sermon, slides, a 4-week small-group study and a workshop outline are all available. See the "For Churches" section on the home page, or contact us directly.

The companion offers general, Scripture-grounded encouragement on family and technology — it is a helper, not a counsellor or pastor. For personal crises it will gently point you to a trusted person. We'd encourage parents to use it alongside, never instead of, real Christian community.

Begin with our "Start Here" guide — it walks you gently through one first step. You do not need to change everything tonight. One stone, laid today, is enough to begin.
