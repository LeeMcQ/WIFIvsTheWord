The Wi-Fi vs The Word — Notes & Study

The full outline of the sermon that began this ministry, with Scripture references and questions for personal or small-group study.

Theme: In our homes, is the Wi-Fi winning, or is the Word? This is not a call to throw away our phones — it is a call to take them captive for Christ.

"Do not be conformed to this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind." — Romans 12:2

1. The Distraction Dilemma

The story of Mary and Martha (Luke 10:38–42). Martha was "distracted by much serving" — the Greek merimnao means to be pulled in different directions. We are physically present with our families, but our minds are pulled away. This is "technoference" — the intrusion of technology into our relationships.

Key thought: If the enemy can keep you scrolling with many things, he can keep you from the one thing — readiness for Christ.

2. The Battle for the Mind

"By beholding we become changed." We cannot consume the world for hours each day and expect the mind of Christ. The major traps: comparison on social media, the dopamine of online shopping, the algorithm, and AI companions that isolate the young from real connection.

"The greatest want of the world is the want of men... who will not be bought or sold." — Education

3. Relationships & the Algorithm

David and Bathsheba (2 Samuel 11). David fell because he did not guard his mind in private. A look became a lingering, a lingering became a choice. Today the rooftop is a screen. The algorithm is not neutral — it is a mirror of our secret attention.

4. The Digital Wall

Nehemiah refused to "come down" from his great work (Nehemiah 6:3). Parents are doing a great work too — raising citizens for heaven. We must build a wall against ungodly content, the slot-machine pull of apps, and the loss of deep work.

5. Rebuilding the Altar — Seven Stones

Before fire fell, Elijah repaired the broken altar (1 Kings 18:30). The seven practical stones: a sacred table and phone-free zones; the Digital Sabbath; replace, don't just remove; the "flee" principle; wait until 8th; the renewing of the mind; and applying the Philippians 4:8 filter.

Questions for Study

  • Where in our home does technology most often "pull us in different directions"?
  • What is the "one thing" our family most needs to protect?
  • Which of the seven stones would be the easiest for us to lay first? Which the hardest?
  • What would a Digital Sabbath realistically look like in our home?
  • How can we make our home so engaging that screens lose their grip?
