The Wi-Fi vs The Word

A story about attention, family, and the quiet battle being fought in every home. Scroll slowly. Let it unfold.

Begin the journey
Scroll
Chapter One

Present in body. Absent in mind.

We sit in the same room, under the same roof — yet each of us is somewhere else. A glance at a screen becomes a minute. A minute becomes an evening. We call it being connected. Our families feel it as something else.

"Martha, Martha, you are worried and distracted by many things; but one thing is needful."

Chapter Two

It was built to be hard to put down.

The pull you feel is not weakness. The feed refreshes like a slot machine — designed, tested, and tuned to hold you. Every notification is a small tug on the mind, training you to return, and return again.

"By beholding we become changed."

Chapter Three

"I am doing a great work. I cannot come down."

When Nehemiah rebuilt the wall, his enemies called him down to the plain again and again. Four times he refused. Every home needs a wall — not to imprison, but to protect what is precious inside. You are doing a great work too.

"So I sent messengers to them, saying, I am doing a great work, so that I cannot come down."

Chapter Four

Rebuild the altar that was broken down.

Before the fire fell on Mount Carmel, Elijah did something quiet first: he repaired the altar that lay in ruins. Restoration begins not with a grand gesture, but with one stone, laid today. A phone-free meal. An evening of presence. A return to the Word together.

"And he repaired the altar of the Lord that was broken down."
