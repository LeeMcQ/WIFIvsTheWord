Mary, Martha, and the Distracted Heart: Choosing the One Thing That Changes Everything

How Jesus’ gentle invitation to Martha speaks directly to our distracted, notification-filled lives — and shows us a better way forward for the whole family.

6 min read· Digital Discipleship

If there’s a Bible story that feels like it was written for the age of notifications, it’s the one about Mary and Martha. You know the scene: Jesus walks into their home, and instantly two different postures emerge. Martha is in the kitchen, multitasking like a pro — meal prep, hospitality logistics, probably checking the bread and stirring the stew. Mary, meanwhile, is sitting at Jesus’ feet, drinking in every word. Both love Jesus. Both want to honour Him. But only one walks away with the quiet, unshakeable peace that He called “the better part.”

If you’re a parent today, you probably feel like Martha most mornings — except your “kitchen” has been replaced by school runs, work emails, WhatsApp groups, meal planning, and that endless scroll before bed. Technology promised us ease, but too often it just adds more “many things” to our already crowded minds. The real question isn’t whether we love Jesus. The question is: what is discipling our attention — the Word or the endless Wi-Fi current?

"As Jesus and his disciples were on their way, he came to a village where a woman named Martha opened her home to him. She had a sister called Mary, who sat at the Lord’s feet listening to what he said. But Martha was distracted by all the preparations that had to be made." — Luke 10:38-40 (NIV)

The Real Problem Isn’t the Kitchen — It’s the Heart

Notice that Jesus doesn’t condemn Martha’s work. Hospitality matters, feeding people is holy, and running a household is good work. The issue isn’t the dishes or the dinner — it’s the distracted heart that becomes anxious and troubled. The Greek word used here, periespato, means “dragged away” or “pulled in different directions.” Sound familiar? In our homes, the “many things” aren’t just chores — they’re the pull of breaking news, the ping of a new like, the subtle pressure to answer every message instantly. Before we know it, we’ve been pulled away from the feet of Jesus.

“Martha, Martha,” the Lord answered, “you are worried and upset about many things, but few things are needed — or indeed only one. Mary has chosen what is better, and it will not be taken away from her.” — Luke 10:41-42

That double call — “Martha, Martha” — is full of compassion, not criticism. Jesus sees her heart. He knows she loves Him, but He also knows that her love is getting tangled in a web of worry. And the same thing happens to us when we let devices and duties crowd out stillness. Technology is a tool, never a tyrant. But when it becomes the first voice we hear in the morning and the last glow we see at night, it starts discipling us — shaping our anxieties, shortening our patience, and fragmenting our presence with the people right in front of us.

Rediscovering the ‘One Thing’ in a Wi-Fi World

Here’s the beautiful truth: Jesus still extends the same invitation today. He doesn’t ask you to throw away your phone or abandon your responsibilities. He simply whispers, “Come, sit with Me. Just for a moment. Let the notifications wait. I want to give you something no scroll can offer.” The “one thing” is not a complicated formula — it’s the posture of a child who stops striving long enough to listen. For tired parents, this can feel impossible. But even five minutes of undivided presence with Jesus reshapes the rest of your day.

In our ministry, we often say: “The issue is not merely screens. The issue is discipleship.” Who — or what — is forming your inner world? Mary chose the better part, and Jesus said it would never be taken from her. Not even the busiest season or the most demanding job can steal the peace of a heart anchored at His feet. That’s why we created the Digital Sabbath reset — a weekly rhythm to step away from the noise and remember that we belong to God before we belong to anyone’s inbox.

Two Simple Steps for Weary Parents (That Actually Work)

You don’t need a dramatic tech detox to start. Jesus meets us in the small, faithful choices. Here are two doable actions any tired mom or dad can try this week:

  • The ‘First Five’ Morning Ritual: Before you check email, social media, or news, set a timer for five minutes. Sit somewhere quiet (even the edge of your bed). Read one verse — Luke 10:41-42 is perfect — and then just breathe and talk to Jesus. No performance, no guilt. Just five minutes of “Mary time.” After three days, you’ll notice the difference in how you respond to your kids’ interruptions.
  • One Device-Free Meal Anchor: Choose one meal today — breakfast, lunch, or dinner — where all phones go into a drawer or another room. Ask each person (including yourself) to share one joy and one struggle from the day. That’s it. Presence over perfection. You’re not banning technology; you’re reclaiming the sacred art of being fully with the people Jesus gave you.

These tiny resets reorient your heart. Over time, they become the rhythm that protects your family from the slow drift of digital distraction. You’ll still cook, clean, and answer messages — but you’ll do it from a place of rest, not restlessness.

So today, if you feel pulled in a dozen directions — if the Wi-Fi is always on and your spirit feels fractured — hear Jesus saying your name with the same tenderness: “[Your name], [your name], you are worried about so many things. But only one thing is essential. Come. Sit. Choose the better part, and it will never be taken from you.” The dishes will wait. The notifications will still be there. But your heart — and your family — will be anchored in the only presence that truly satisfies.

"But the Lord answered her, 'Martha, Martha, you are anxious and troubled about many things, but one thing is necessary. Mary has chosen the good portion, which will not be taken away from her.'" — Luke 10:41-42 (ESV)
